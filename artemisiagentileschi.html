Artemisia Gentileschi

Artemisia Gentileschi, a 17th century artist, is considered among one of the most accomplished painters of that time. She is also the first woman to become a member of the Academy of the Arts of Drawing in Florence. Some of her patrons include members of nobility, including the House of Medici, the Grand Duke of Tuscany, and King Philip IV of Spain. Some of her most famous works include: Self Portrait as the Allegory of Painting, Lucretia, and Susanna and the Elders.
